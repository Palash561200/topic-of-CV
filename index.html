Titration

A titration is a technique where a solution of known concentration is used to determine the concentration of unknown solution.

  1. Typically,the titrant (the known sol)
  2. is added from buret
  3. to a known quantity
  4. of the analyte unknown solution
  5. until the reaction complete

Colorimetric Assay

The principle of this method lies in the generation of color by chemical reaction btween target analyte and reagents.
The most significant advantage of the Colorimetric assay on paper is its simplicity.

Ex. Its are often used for protein analysis.

Antibiogram

An Antibiogram is an overall profile of microbial susceptibility testing results of a specific micro-organism to a battery of antimicrobial drug.

Uses for

  1. help clinician and pharmacist in selecting best antimicrobial treatment.
  2. Useful tools for detecting and monitoring in antimicrobial resistantce.

Test done by

  1. Sample collect from patient
  2. sent in lab
  3. test, antibiotics of various levels of concentration.
    4. To see how much of the drug is needed to kill the pathogen.

Basic Immunochemistry

Immunochemistry is the study of the chemistry of immune system. This involves the study of the properties,function,interaction and production of the chemical components (antibodys/immunoglobulins,toxin,epitopes of protein like CD4,antitoxin,chytokines,antigen) of the immine system.
